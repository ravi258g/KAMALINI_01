Parsi Gara

Intro & History: Parsi Gara embroidery is a unique and endangered craft developed by the Parsi community in the 19th century. It emerged when Parsi traders brought Chinese silk embroidery (gaaj) to India via trade routes. Parsis adapted these techniques, blending Chinese motifs with Persian and Indian elements, creating a distinctive style that became a hallmark of Parsi identity and luxury.

Significance & Work: The embroidery is done by hand on fine silk or crepe using satin stitch and French knots, featuring intricate floral, bird, butterfly, and vine patterns in rich colors. Each saree can take months to complete due to the density and precision required. Gara sarees are treasured heirlooms, worn during Navjote ceremonies, weddings, and Parsi festivals. They symbolize cultural fusion, refinement, and the Parsi community's resilience and artistic legacy — elegant, delicate, and deeply meaningful.
